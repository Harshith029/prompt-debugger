# M1 — Knowledge Verification & Policy Authoring (Product Specification)

|---|---|
| **Milestone** | M1 |
| **Type** | Product specification (not an implementation plan) |
| **Status** | Draft — awaiting approval; no work has begun |
| **Predecessor** | [M0 — Engineering Foundation](../docs/releases/M0.md) (frozen) |

> This document describes *what* M1 must deliver and *how success is judged*. It deliberately contains no implementation detail, no code design, and no analyzer or rewrite logic. Those remain out of scope until later milestones.

## Background

M0 delivered the engineering foundation: versioned contracts, the Knowledge Engine structure, tooling, and CI. The Knowledge Engine ships with **seed content** — a claim registry, techniques, an observable-event taxonomy, a rubric, and pattern stubs — but that content is provisional: every factual claim carries `status: "recorded"` (captured, not yet verified against its live source), and techniques and taxonomy entries are `status: "draft"`. The `common` pack also lacks the misuse policy, rewrite-transformation rules, and fixed notice texts that the contracts already reference.

The product's entire value proposition rests on *sourced accuracy* and a *specified, testable no-bypass policy*. Neither can be trusted while the knowledge is unverified and the policy is unwritten.

## Problem statement

The foundation cannot support any user-facing analysis or rewrite feature until:

1. every claim about provider behavior is verified against its current public source (or retired), and
2. the misuse/legitimacy policy, rewrite transformation rules, and fixed notices exist as authored, versioned, testable knowledge — not as contract placeholders.

Until then, later milestones would be building analysis on assertions that may be stale and a safety boundary that is only described, not defined.

## Goals

- Verify every entry in the Anthropic claim registry against its live public source and record the outcome.
- Promote verified knowledge from provisional (`recorded`/`draft`) to active status where it holds; retire or revise what does not.
- Author the provider-neutral **misuse policy**, **rewrite-transformation rules**, and **fixed notices** in the `common` pack, matching the semantics the contracts already specify.
- Expand the observable-event taxonomy's "can conclude / cannot conclude" prose to production quality and resolve the open `clm-consumer-surface-fallback` question.
- Populate the pattern library so each pattern is a complete, accurate before/after example.
- Preserve the provenance chain end to end: every provider statement remains tied to a dated, verified claim.

## Non-goals

- Implementing the analyzer, rewriter, storage, redaction, verification, or rendering components (M2–M4).
- Changing any contract, schema, or the repository layout (M0 is frozen).
- Adding new providers or new knowledge-pack *types*.
- Building evaluation harnesses or measuring model behavior.

## User stories

- *As a user whose legitimate prompt was declined,* I want the explanation of what I observed to cite accurate, current public documentation, so I can trust it is not folklore.
- *As a maintainer,* I want every claim to show when it was last verified and against which source, so I can audit the knowledge and re-verify on a schedule.
- *As a reviewer,* I want the no-bypass policy written down as explicit allowed/prohibited transformations, so I can confirm the safety boundary is real and testable.
- *As a contributor,* I want the pattern library to contain complete examples, so I understand the intended quality bar for rewrites without reading source code.

## Functional requirements

1. **Claim verification.** Each registry claim is checked against its `url`; its `status` becomes `verified` (holds), `stale` (source changed — needs revision), or `retired` (no longer supported). `last_verified` is set. The `clm-consumer-surface-fallback` question is resolved to one of these states.
2. **Status promotion.** Techniques and taxonomy entries whose supporting claims are all `verified` may become `active`; any citing a non-active claim must not be `active` (invariant KN-2).
3. **Policy authoring.** The `common` pack gains the misuse decision procedure, the rewrite allowed/prohibited transformation rules, and the fixed epistemic and non-guarantee notice texts, consistent with the Rewrite Report and knowledge contracts.
4. **Taxonomy prose.** Every event-taxonomy entry has complete, accurate `can_conclude` / `cannot_conclude` text and correct `api_correlate`, reflecting verification outcomes.
5. **Pattern completion.** Every pattern referenced by the index has an authored, accurate body; there are no dangling references (invariant KN-6).
6. **Version bookkeeping.** Knowledge-pack versions are bumped to reflect the content change, and the change is recorded in the changelog.

## Non-functional requirements

- **Provenance:** no provider statement without a claim citation (KN-1); no active entry citing a non-active claim (KN-2).
- **Auditability:** verification outcomes and dates are recorded in the registry, and the quarterly re-verification process remains runnable.
- **Consistency:** all knowledge-integrity tests continue to pass; the `common` pack stays provider-neutral (KN-5).
- **No regression:** every M0 quality gate remains green.

## Constraints

- M0 contracts, schemas, structure, and layout are frozen; M1 changes **content within** the existing Knowledge Engine, not its shape.
- Runtime remains standard-library only; no runtime network I/O is added (verification is a human/authoring activity recorded in data, not a runtime feature).
- Knowledge files remain JSON with Markdown companions; the schema subset and existing validators are unchanged.

## Architecture impact

None structural. M1 edits data and prose within `core/knowledge/` and adds authored policy/notice files to the `common` pack that the contracts already anticipate. No new components, no contract or schema changes. If verification uncovers an objective contract defect, that fix follows the frozen-baseline exception and is recorded.

## Deliverables

- A fully verified Anthropic claim registry (every claim `verified`, `stale`, or `retired`, with dates).
- Active-status techniques, rubric, and taxonomy where support holds.
- Authored `common`-pack misuse policy, rewrite-transformation rules, and notices.
- Complete taxonomy prose and a resolved consumer-surface-fallback claim.
- A complete pattern library (no stubs, no dangling references).
- Updated knowledge-pack versions and changelog entry; a milestone record `docs/releases/M1.md` at close-out.

## Acceptance criteria

- No claim remains in `status: "recorded"`; each is `verified`, `stale`, or `retired` with a `last_verified` date.
- No `active` technique or taxonomy entry cites a non-active claim.
- The `common` pack contains the misuse policy, rewrite rules, and notice texts, and they match the contract semantics.
- Every indexed pattern resolves to an authored file.
- All existing quality gates pass; knowledge-integrity tests (including KN-1, KN-2, KN-5, KN-6) pass.

## Test strategy

- Extend the existing knowledge-integrity tests to assert the promoted provenance rules (active-cites-active) hold across the now-active corpus.
- Add existence/shape checks for the new `common`-pack policy and notice files so their presence and references are enforced.
- Re-run the full M0 gate unchanged; M1 introduces content, not runtime code, so no new runtime tests are required.
- Verification itself is evidenced in the registry data (URLs + dates), reviewable during independent verification.

## Risks

- **Source drift:** public documentation may have changed since capture; mitigated by recording `stale` and revising rather than silently trusting.
- **Over-claiming during authoring:** the misuse policy or notices could overstate certainty; mitigated by keeping them tied to the honesty architecture and reviewing against the contracts.
- **Scope creep into M2:** authoring may tempt implementation of the analyzer; mitigated by the explicit non-goals and the frozen baseline.

## Out of scope

Analyzer/rewrite/storage implementation; evaluation harnesses; new providers; contract/schema/layout changes; UI or packaging work.

## Definition of done

M1 is done when the acceptance criteria are met, all quality gates pass, an independent verification has approved the milestone, `docs/releases/M1.md` records it, and the Knowledge Engine content is frozen as the verified baseline for M2.
